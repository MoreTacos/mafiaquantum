Theoretical Physics is unapproachable to most people

Mix the Feynman technique with Socratic seminars and that changes

This is the best way to learn.

Richard Feynman was one of the best theoretical physicist alive. His trick for learning physics? The Feynman technique: explaining to others. You don't understand a concept properly until you can explain it easily.

We engineer an environment where you explain to others, and these others challenge your explanation.

We listen to Frederic Schuller’s lectures on first-principles theoretical physics. You get paired with a group of people who are on the same lecture as you. Every week you listen to a new lecture and on weekends you meet.

Check out some of our past sessions on YouTube for more context.

FAQ

How will this help me with Quantum Computing? I expect a course on quantum mechanics to cover uncertainty, particles/waves, harmonic oscillator, time evolution of hamiltonians etc.

You'll only get a surface level understanding of physics by learning that. To get a deep understanding it goes like this:

logic -> set theory -> topology -> topological manifolds -> differentiable manifolds -> bundles -> symp./metric geometry -> quantum mechanics!

Foundations is not mutually exclusive with the flashy stuff. But eventually you will need to learn it. Why not learn it like this? It’s way more fun than reading textbooks yourself.

University students spend years learning these subjects - how am I going to learn this so quickly?

The difference is that with the method you actually learn. It's very easy to get the impression that you learned something. But really a few weeks later you forget it all. Having to explain the concept to somebody else forces you to internalize it.

Also, this group is a forcing function for constant exposure to mathematics. We’re going at a pace of 1 session/week, or around 3h/week. That means by the end of the year members will expose themselves to on average over 150 hours of math. Constant exposure is so much better then cramming for a test!

And it’s also a first principles approach to math. It all builds up from pure logic hence with enough hard work everything will click.

I don't have time for 3h/week.

When something is important, you make time for it. If this is not a priority, that's cool.

This is a lot closer to a fiction reading club than a rigorous course. It will be enjoyable to learn math using this format. Spending hours won’t even bother you.
